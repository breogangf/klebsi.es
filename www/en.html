Hospital pathogens:

Nosocomial bacteria are those that cause infections in a hospital setting and are more difficult to treat. They are usually opportunists because they take advantage of imbalances in the body to colonize an individual.

Currently there is a group of bacteria called ESKAPE that cause serious problems in the hospital setting due to the high rate of antibiotic resistance they present. They are considered superbugs and their name arises by playing with the ability to escape from microbial agents.

Klebsiella Pneumoniae

  • General characteristics:

    Klebsiella pneumoniae belongs to the family Enterobacteriaceae. It has the shape of a bacillus and lives in the environment (soil and water), and in the gastrointestinal tract of animals, which is why it is called an enteric.

    It is a bacteria associated with various types of infections especially nosocomial, such as urinary infection, sepsis, pneumonia, meningitis... The colonization of the bacteria occurs by contact with contaminated people or objects.

  • Mechanisms of resistance:

    Generally the resistances that can have are related to:

    • β-lactamase enzymes: are proteins encoded in plasmids that inactivate β-lactam antibiotics, which inhibit the formation of the bacterial cell wall.

    • Resistance to quinolones: sthey are mutations in the bacterial chromosome that contribute resistance to these antibiotics by means of a change in the target of the drug.

    • Resistance to aminoglycosides: they are genes located in a plasmid that make proteins that inactivate antibiotics because they modify the target.

  • Urinary infection:

    The urinary infection can affect the bladder, ureters and kidney due to the presence of various pathogenic microorganisms. It can be more complicated when the bacteria reach the blood causing sepsis (general infection).

    The antibiotics that are usually used in this case are β-lactams or aminoglycosides; but when they do not work, the last-resort ones, such as quinolones, are used. Current problems with antibiotics:

Currently there is no effective antibiotic against multiresistant Klebsiella pneumoniae and ESKAPE bacteria, this is due to the difficulty of discovering new antibiotics, as well as to the little economic investment made by pharmaceutical companies.

To improve this problem, an appropriate policy and responsible use and consumption should be established, based on the guidelines indicated by the World Health Organization (WHO). This is very important because if we continue along this path, in 2050 antibiotics will be ineffective and resistance to antibiotics will be the 1st cause of death in the world, surpassing cancer. 1 death every 3 seconds !!
